Dublin Castle

Dublin Castle, a historic landmark in the heart of Ireland’s capital, has played a central role in the country's history for over 800 years. Originally built as a medieval fortress under King John of England in 1204, it later became the seat of British rule in Ireland until 1922. The castle’s architecture reflects its long history, featuring medieval, Georgian, and Victorian elements. One of its most striking features is the Record Tower, the only surviving structure from the original medieval castle. The State Apartments, once the residence of British viceroys, now serve as a venue for state functions, including presidential inaugurations. The castle also houses the Chester Beatty Library, which contains a world-renowned collection of manuscripts, rare books, and artworks. Dublin Castle was the site of numerous historic events, including the handover of power to the newly formed Irish Free State in 1922. Today, it is a major tourist attraction and a symbol of Ireland’s evolution from colonial rule to independence. Visitors can explore its grand halls, exhibitions, and beautiful gardens, making it a must-see destination for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike.
